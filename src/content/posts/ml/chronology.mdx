#

## 1895 – A Humble beginning in a deeply rooted faith

* Born on February 25, 1895, in Kuravilangad, Travancore, into the ancient Pakalomattom-Parekunnel family, tracing its Christian roots to the earliest converts by St. Thomas the Apostle.

## 1918-1924 - A Brilliant Scholar

* B,A (Hons) from St. Joseph’s College, Trichy, Graduated with First rank and first class with record marks from Madras University.
* Completed his B.Litt. (1922) and D.Phil. (1924) in Economics from Oxford University, among the first Indian economists to earn a doctorate from Oxford.
* His research, focused on the Indian calico trade, became a foundation for later studies in colonial trade and mercantilism.

## 1924–1942 – Professor, Writer, Reformer

* Taught at the University of Ceylon, then at the University of Madras, where he headed the Economics Department.
* Authored pioneering works on rural indebtedness, prohibition economics, cooperative credit, and federal finance.
* His book The Growth of Federal Finance in India (1939) became a cornerstone of Indian fiscal thought.
* Conducted one of the first rural economic survey in nine villages of South India, gathering systematic data on poverty and debt through on-site field  work—allowing policymakers to understand rural challenges with data and not assumptions.

## 1937 – Economic Statesmanship

* Elected President of the Indian Economic Association, a post later held by Nobel Laureate, Dr.Amartya Sen.
* Nominated to the Madras Legislative Council.
* Economic Advisor to the Madras State under C. Rajagopalachari. During the prohibition experiment in Salem he introduced the concept of sales tax — a model later adopted nationwide.

## 1942–1948 – Architect of Post-War Indian Economic Thinking

* Appointed Economic Adviser to the Government of India — first under British rule, then under Independent India in the Jawaharlal Nehru Government.
* Championed rural employment, public works and cooperative movements.

## 1944-1945 – Represented India on the Global stage

* Attended two landmark international events which resulted in the birth of a new world order
* The Bretton Woods Conference, 1944, which led to the creation of the IMF and the World Bank.
* Delegate at the San Francisco Conference, 1945, which adopted the United Nations Charter, where he helped draft the vision of the third organ of the UN, ECOSOC Council.

Delegates of fifty nations met at San Francisco between April 25 and June 26, 1945. Working on the Dumbarton Oaks proposals, the Yalta Agreement, and amendments proposed by various Governments, the Conference agreed upon the Charter of the United Nations and the Statute of the new International Court of Justice. The Charter was passed unanimously and signed by all the representatives. It came into force on October 24, 1945, when China, France, the USSR, the United Kingdom, and the United States and a majority of the other signatories had filed their instruments of ratification.

## 1950–1952 – Builder of Institutions and Mentor

* Returned to Kerala to become the founding principal of St. Thomas College, Palai — blending academic excellence with moral formation.

## 1957–1962 – A Parliamentary Voice for the Poor

Nominated to the Rajya Sabha by both the ruling Communist Party of India and the Congress. During his tenure he championed for –

* Land reform
* Decentralized planning
* Employment for the rural poor


## 1965 – A Life Well Lived

* Passed away on July 26th, 1965, in Aluva, Kerala.
* Left behind no monuments or riches but a legacy of intellect, integrity, and immense compassion.
